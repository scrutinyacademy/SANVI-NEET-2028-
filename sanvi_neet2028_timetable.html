✅ Daily Task Breakdown

What exactly to do in each study slot · Every single day

📖
TASK 1 — NCERT Reading (Daily)

NCERT for NEET

• Read NCERT line by line — not skimming
• Mark: Green = remember, Yellow = important, Red = difficult
• Read diagrams deeply — draw them from memory
• Target: 1 full NCERT topic/day (Biology), ½ chapter/day (Phy/Chem)
• Re-read marked sections before sleeping

🗒️
TASK 2 — Notes Making (Daily)

Smart Notes for Speed Revision

• Use A4 sheets — one topic one page
• Use colours, arrows, boxes — make it visual
• Biology: flowcharts, diagrams, tables
• Physics: formulas box + solved example
• Chemistry: reactions + conditions table
• Review notes every Sunday — add/edit as you learn more

⏪
TASK 3 — Revision Slots (Daily)

Spaced Repetition System

• Day 1: Learn new topic
• Day 2: Revise same topic (10 mins)
• Day 7: Revise again (5 mins)
• Day 30: Final quick revision
• Use Anki app or flashcards for Biology terms
• Every afternoon slot (2:30–4:30 PM) is REVISION only

📝
TASK 4 — Previous Year Questions (Daily)

NEET PYQs — Topic-wise

• Source: NEET 2013–2024 papers topic-wise
• Do PYQs after completing a topic — not before
• Target: 20–30 PYQs per topic
• Analyse every wrong answer — understand WHY
• Maintain an "Error Log" notebook
• Revise error log every Sunday

🎯
TASK 5 — MCQ Practice (Daily)

Chapter-wise MCQ Drills

• 45 MCQs per session (simulate NEET pace)
• Time yourself: 45 questions in 45 minutes
• Sources: MTG Fingertips, DC Pandey, Errorless
• Review ALL wrong + guessed answers
• Topic MCQs → Chapter MCQs → Mixed MCQs (progression)
• Weekend: 60–90 MCQ full subject mock

🏫
TASK 6 — Intermediate Board (Daily)

Telangana Board Exam Prep

• 11:00 AM–1:00 PM strictly for board topics
• Focus on: Telugu, English, additional subjects
• Practice board answer format (6–8 mark answers)
• Sample papers from TS Board (manabadi style)
• Monthly: 1 full board paper timed (2–3 hrs)
• Don't neglect board — it matters for eligibility!

🌸 Health, Wellness & Mental Peace

Sanvi — your health is your secret weapon. A healthy body = a sharper brain.

🧘

Morning Yoga (15 min)

Surya Namaskar × 5 rounds, Cat-Cow stretch, Child's Pose, Downward Dog. Flexibility + spine health for long study hours.

🌬️

Pranayama (10 min)

Anulom Vilom (5 min) + Bhramari (3 min) + Deep breathing (2 min). Reduces anxiety, sharpens focus, calms nervous system.

🚶

Daily Walk (10 min)

Morning brisk walk in fresh air. Boosts dopamine. Get natural sunlight for Vitamin D. Don't study while walking — just breathe.

😴

Power Nap (15 min)

2:15–2:30 PM daily. Proven to improve afternoon retention by 40%. Set an alarm. Even lying still helps reset your mind.

📓

Gratitude Journal

Every night: write 3 things you're grateful for + 1 thing you learned today. Protects against exam anxiety and burnout.

🎨

Sunday Hobby Hour

Drawing, music, cooking something new, calling a friend — Sunday 2:30–5 PM is fully yours. No guilt. You've earned it!

🍎

Nutrition Tips

Eat fruits daily. Include nuts (almonds, walnuts) for brain health. Avoid heavy meals before study sessions. Warm milk at night.

📵

Screen-Free Wind Down

No phone after 9:30 PM. Blue light kills sleep quality. Replace with light reading, stretching, or quiet family time.
